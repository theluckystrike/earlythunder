## Problem Statement

Most investors discover opportunities after the 10x. By the time something trends on social media, appears in a newsletter, or gets listed on a major exchange, the asymmetric window is closed.

But the signals that preceded every major breakout of the last 15 years were visible months or years before the crowd arrived:

- **Bitcoin (2009):** Open-source commit velocity, cryptography mailing list density, zero mainstream coverage, sub-$1M market cap.
- **Ethereum (2015):** Developer migration from Bitcoin, novel smart-contract primitive, Thiel Fellowship backing, institutional whitepaper citations.
- **DeFi Summer (2020):** TVL inflection, governance token primitives, audited protocol count doubling quarterly, gas fee anomalies signaling organic demand.
- **NFTs (2021):** Creator-economy discourse shift, wallet-creation acceleration, cultural figure participation, marketplace transaction volume curves.

These weren't lucky guesses. They were measurable patterns. The same signal archetypes repeat across asset classes — from biotech to rare earth minerals to decentralized infrastructure.

next1000x tracks these signals systematically, scores them, and surfaces what matches before the narrative takes hold.

## The 8-Signal Framework

We score every opportunity against 8 dimensions that historically preceded asymmetric breakouts. Each signal is scored 0-10. The composite score determines tier placement.

| # | Signal | What It Measures |
|---|--------|-----------------|
| 1 | **Asymmetric Upside** | Market cap vs. addressable market. Room to run, or already priced in? |
| 2 | **Innovation Density** | Novel mechanism, IP moat, or genuine technical primitive. Not a fork. |
| 3 | **Timing & Catalysts** | Regulatory tailwinds, infrastructure readiness, upcoming unlock events. |
| 4 | **Team & Ecosystem** | Builder quality, contributor graphs, institutional backing signals. |
| 5 | **Community & Adoption** | Organic traction metrics — not bot farms. Wallet growth, usage curves, discourse quality. |
| 6 | **Contrarian Signal** | Is the crowd wrong? Narrative discount, misclassification, or active skepticism from consensus. |
| 7 | **Liquidity & Access** | Can you actually get in? Entry mechanisms, exchange availability, lock-up structures. |
| 8 | **Risk-Adjusted Conviction** | Bear case severity, existential risks, correlation to macro. The honest "this could go to zero" score. |

The framework is detailed in full on our [Methodology](/methodology) page, including historical back-testing against known breakouts and known failures.

## FAQ

**What kinds of opportunities does next1000x track?**
We scan across four domains: crypto and blockchain projects, deep technology (AI, robotics, quantum, biotech), physical commodities (rare earths, energy, agriculture), and frontier science (longevity, space, materials science). If it has asymmetric upside potential and measurable pre-breakout signals, it's in scope.

**How is this different from a crypto newsletter or stock-pick service?**
We don't make predictions or issue buy signals. next1000x is a structured research database powered by a repeatable scoring framework. We surface what matches the pattern. You decide whether and how to act. Every entry includes a bear case and an explicit "this could go to zero" risk assessment.

**How often is the database updated?**
The core database runs on a weekly automation cycle. Signal scores are recalculated every 7 days. Major catalyst events or material changes trigger interim updates. Historical scores are preserved so you can track trajectory over time.

**What does "Tier 1 — Pre-Narrative" actually mean?**
Tier 1 means the opportunity exhibits strong signal scores but has not yet entered mainstream discourse. There is no active narrative. Coverage is minimal. This is the highest-asymmetry, highest-risk window. Most Tier 1 entries will fail. The ones that don't tend to produce the largest returns.

**Can I lose money following what's in the database?**
Yes. Absolutely. Many of the opportunities we track — especially Tier 1 and Tier 2 entries — are early-stage, illiquid, and carry existential risk. Historical pattern matching does not guarantee future performance. The database includes a graveyard of opportunities that scored well and still went to zero. Position sizing and risk management are your responsibility.

**Do you have any affiliation with the projects you list?**
No. next1000x does not accept payment for listings, does not hold advisory positions, and discloses any token/equity positions held by the team. Our revenue comes exclusively from subscriptions and API access.

**Is there a refund policy?**
Yes. If you subscribe to Pro and find it isn't useful within the first 14 days, email us for a full refund. No questions.

**What's the API rate limit and data format?**
The API returns JSON. Rate limit is 1,000 requests per day. Endpoints cover the full opportunity database, individual signal scores, historical score timelines, and graveyard entries. Full API documentation is provided upon subscription.
