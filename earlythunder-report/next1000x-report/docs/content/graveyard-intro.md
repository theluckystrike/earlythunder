# The Graveyard

## Tracking failures teaches more than tracking wins.

Every opportunity in the next1000x database could go to zero. Many of them will.

The Graveyard is where those outcomes live. It contains every opportunity that once scored 6.0 or higher on our composite framework and subsequently failed — defined as a 90%+ sustained drawdown, project abandonment, regulatory shutdown, or confirmed fraud.

We don't hide these. We study them.

Each Graveyard entry preserves the original signal scores from when the opportunity was first identified, alongside a post-mortem analysis: what went wrong, which signals were misleading, and whether there were early warning indicators that our framework missed or underweighted.

Why publish this? Because a scoring framework that only shows you what worked is worse than useless — it builds false confidence. The Graveyard is a corrective. It shows you what "strong signals, wrong outcome" actually looks like. It reveals the failure modes that recur: team collapses, tokenomics death spirals, regulatory crackdowns, technology that never shipped, markets that simply didn't care.

If you study the Graveyard carefully, you'll start to see patterns in the failures too. Not all 7.0-composite opportunities are created equal. Some signal profiles carry hidden fragility — high Innovation Density paired with low Team & Ecosystem, for example, or strong Asymmetric Upside with no Liquidity & Access, which means no exit when things go wrong.

The Graveyard is available to all Pro subscribers. We consider it one of the most valuable sections of the platform.

If you want a database that only tells you good news, this isn't it.
