## Methodology Notes

### Scoring Adjustments for Non-Crypto Assets
For traditional equities, fusion, quantum, and biotech entries, the "Developer Activity Surge" signal was reinterpreted as "Production/Project Development Activity" to maintain relevance. The "Hard to Buy" signal for private companies reflects the inability for retail investors to access these opportunities directly.

### Key Caveats
1. Scores are estimates based on publicly available information as of April 2026.
2. Market caps are approximate and fluctuate daily.
3. "Toy Phase Dismissal" is inherently subjective -- it measures the ratio of dismissive to serious coverage.
4. Private companies score high on "Hard to Buy" but this can be a double-edged sword: it is an adoption moat but also prevents accumulation.
5. No financial advice is provided or implied by these scores.

### Data Sources
Research compiled from web searches across CoinMarketCap, Messari, Blockworks, The Block, CoinGecko, Seeking Alpha, TechCrunch, Motley Fool, VanEck, Sprott, and project-specific documentation.
